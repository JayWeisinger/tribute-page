Wes Montgomery

Jazz's hardest working musician

Wes and his guitar


Wes Montgomery was one of the most influential guitarists of the twentieth century. A performer, bandleader, lifelong musician and grammy award winning recording artist. He had an active career from the year 1947 until his untimely death in 1968. His work spanned across several genres of jazz including smooth jazz, hard bop, post bop, jazz-soul and jazz-fusion. His performances and recordings have been well received and highly praised by numerous publications including: Billboard, Metronome, DownBeat and Record World who awarded him Jazz Man of the Year. His distinctive style and techniques of playing set him apart from his contemporaries. Wes didn't play the guitar with a pick, instead using solely the side of his thumb to play fast paced sequences and block chords patterns. This technique made his sound incredibly smooth and easy on the ears while still maintaining a measured intensity.


Short summary of Wes Montgomery's time on earth:

  • 1923 

    - Born in Indianapolis, Indiana. Montgomery and his brothers moved to Columbus, Ohio after his parents' divorce with their father.

  • 1935 

    - Gifted his first guitar, a four-string tenor, by his older brother Monk.

  • 1943 

    - After returning to Indianapolis with his brothers, Wes started working as a welder and married his wife.

  • 1948 

    - While playing nightclubs in Indianapolis he was hired by bandleader Lionel Hampton to play guitar after hearing Montgomery play like Charlie Christian.

  • 1957 

    - Formed "The Mastersounds" with his two brothers and signed a recording contract at Pacific Jazz and released two albums.

  • 1960 

    - Recorded "A Dynamic New Sound, the Wes Montgomery Trio" this was his first album as bandleader after twenty years of being a musician. As well as recording "The Incredible Jazz Guitar of Wes Montgomery" with his new label Riverside Records.

  • 1961 

    - Performed at the Monterey Jazz Festival in California with The Montgomery Brothers trio. Signed to Fantasy records and released the album "Groove Yard". Montgomery also played with the John Coltrane Band.

  • 1962 

    -  Recorded the album "Full House" in California with musicians Johnny Griffin, Wynton Kelly, Paul Chambers, and Jimmy Cobb.

  • 1968 

    - Died from a heart attack in hometown of Indianapolis at the age of 45.
